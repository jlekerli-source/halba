AI agents are getting faster at changing code. But the expensive question remains: can a claim of done actually pass human review?

Halba is a local-first evidence control plane. It imports an agent report, source files, diffs, and machine receipts into one bounded proof packet.

GPT-5.6 Sol, at max reasoning, extracts atomic claims and precise citations. Then Halba checks the actual bytes. Deterministic guards own paths, line ranges, dates, receipts, and explicit contradictions.

Here, the model claims the analysis was live. The execution receipt says recorded. Halba marks the contradiction and opens the exact lines.

This is the real Build Week patch for the stale review clock. The diff, boundary tests, hash, model reasoning boundary, and guard result stay visible together.

Claims that remain unsupported, stale, contradictory, or uncertain enter a human gate. Approve, reject, or resolve; the decision stays local.
